Six-Step Suitability Workflow

1
Criteria Definition

Stakeholder workshops to define criteria, constraints, and weight priorities

2
Data Assembly

Raster and vector datasets acquired from authoritative sources, reprojected to common CRS

3
Normalization

Each raster reclassified to common score range (1–9 or 0–1). Fuzzy membership or standard reclassification

4
Constraint Masking

Binary exclusion masks applied before weighted sum: protected areas, flood high zones, steep slopes

5
Weighted Overlay

ArcGIS Spatial Analyst Weighted Overlay or raster algebra: Σ(layer_score × weight)

6
Candidate Ranking & Validation

Top-N candidate locations ranked, validated against field survey and stakeholder review

Client-Provided Layers

Client-Provided GIS Data

The following layer types are required from the client or national data custodian. GeoElite Sphere does not substitute these with generic or invented data.

  • Cadastral parcel boundaries and land ownership records
  • Municipal utility network geometry (water supply, sewer, electrical distribution, telecom)
  • Building permits, planning applications, and zoning files
  • Facility asset registries and maintenance history (COBie / EAM exports)
  • Live IoT and SCADA sensor telemetry for digital twin deployments
  • National active fault catalogues (for site-specific PSHA)
  • Classified or restricted infrastructure locations
  • Survey GPS control points for BIM georeferencing
Seismic Fault Line Policy: This platform does not display seismic fault lines unless validated active fault geometry from the GEM Global Active Faults Database or a national geological survey is specifically loaded for a client engagement. Fault line delineation requires a peer-reviewed active fault catalogue — not a generic polygon layer. For probabilistic seismic hazard analysis (PSHA), GeoElite Sphere references GEM OpenQuake Engine methodology and the EMSC regional seismicity catalogue.
